# Prior Authorization & Compliance Rules — State: CA
### Reference document for the Cross-Lens Reconciliation Agent

## Procedures Requiring Prior Authorization

| Procedure Code | Description | Prior Auth Required | Typical Turnaround |
|---|---|---|---|
| 70551 | MRI, brain, without contrast | Yes | 3-5 business days |
| 27447 | Total knee arthroplasty | Yes | 5-7 business days |
| 96413 | Chemotherapy administration, IV | Yes | 1-2 business days (expedited) |

## The Compliance Rule the Cross-Lens Agent Must Enforce

**A claim for a procedure requiring prior authorization, submitted
without an approved authorization on file, cannot be auto-approved —
regardless of what the Policy Validation or Adjudication agents
concluded about coverage.** This is a hard rule, not a judgment call.

This is deliberately the same rule structure as the policy coverage
schedule's "covered but requires prior auth" note — the two documents
describe the same real-world contradiction from two different angles
(what's covered vs. what's compliant), which is exactly why a single
agent reasoning over only one of them can miss it, and why a dedicated
cross-lens check matters.

## Timely Filing

Claims must be submitted within 180 days of the date of service. A
claim submitted after this window should be flagged for human review
regardless of coverage determination — timely filing exceptions
sometimes apply (e.g., retroactive eligibility) but require human
judgment, not automatic denial or approval.

## High-Dollar-Value Review Threshold

**Any claim with a billed amount of $5,000 or more requires human
sign-off before payment, regardless of how clean the automated
adjudication looks.** This is a standard organizational risk-control
practice, independent of medical necessity or coverage correctness —
high-dollar claims get a second set of eyes purely because of the
financial exposure, not because anything about the claim itself looks
wrong.
